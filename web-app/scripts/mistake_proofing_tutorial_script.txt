The Mistake Proofing report uses a unique system to identify and assess potential failures. Notice that instead of "Categories", the system uses "Potential Failures" for this report type.

Unlike other reports, Mistake Proofing doesn't have default categories. You'll create custom potential failures specific to your process or product.

To add a potential failure, click the plus button. Enter the name of the potential failure you want to assess, such as:
- Incorrect Part Selection
- Cross-threading of Fasteners
- Electrical Connection Error
- Missing Quality Inspection
- Tool Calibration Failure
- Documentation Error

When you create a potential failure, the system automatically adds two assessment statements:

**Potential Score**: How likely is it that this failure will happen?
- 1 = Very unlikely (rare occurrence)
- 2 = Unlikely (occasional occurrence)
- 3 = Possible (moderate occurrence)
- 4 = Likely (frequent occurrence)
- 5 = Very likely (almost certain)

**Consequences Score**: How severe would the impact be if this failure occurred?
- 1 = Minimal impact (no safety or quality concern)
- 2 = Minor impact (slight quality issue)
- 3 = Moderate impact (noticeable quality issue)
- 4 = Major impact (significant quality or safety concern)
- 5 = Critical impact (severe safety risk or total failure)

The system calculates a total score for each potential failure by adding the Potential and Consequences scores, helping you prioritize which failures need immediate attention.

Note that you cannot add additional statements to potential failures - the system only uses these two standardized assessment criteria.

In the Additional Details section for each potential failure, you can:
- Add detailed descriptions of the failure mode
- Upload photos of existing mistake-proofing solutions
- Document poka-yoke devices or procedures
- Specify detection methods and controls
- Tag relevant team members

You can't rearrange potential failures, but you can organize them by creation order or priority.

The radar chart visualization shows your assessment scores across all potential failures, making it easy to identify high-risk areas that need mistake-proofing solutions.

Each potential failure card displays:
- The failure name
- Total score (sum of Potential and Consequences)
- Average score
- Color-coded risk level

The system helps you prioritize mistake-proofing efforts based on the combination of likelihood and severity, following standard risk assessment practices.

Finally, you can export to PDF and get yourself a comprehensive Mistake Proofing report with risk assessments, poka-yoke solutions, and implementation recommendations.

The PDF export provides a professional format perfect for quality reviews, process improvement meetings, and documenting your error prevention strategies.
