Hello and welcome to LeanFit Pro. Here we'll learn how to create a Gemba Walk report.

First, login to LeanFit and you'll see a menu. This opens a mega menu for the application. Locate the Reports option here and click on it.

Now you can see the reports page with a Create Report button. Let's click on it.

You can add a report name here and select Gemba Walk from the report type dropdown menu. We have 21 report types available.

You can also assign this report to a project and assign it to organization members.

Let's create the Gemba Walk report now.

Once it's created, you can see the video tutorial from here. Click on Edit to edit the report details, such as the report name. Note that you cannot change the report type once it's created. Everything else remains the same, and you can update the details.

You can also copy a report with all its data.

Additionally, you can mark the report as completed. When you do this, the system will reward you with awards according to your organization's configuration.

Now let's get started with the Gemba Walk report. Click on Open to open the report.

Now, this is the Action Item section where you can create action items for this report. Click here and the action item creation modal will appear. You can add a title, description, and set a due date for the action item. The report is already selected. You can add members if the report has assignees - they will show here.

You can add images to your action item, then create it. You've successfully created an action item.

The Gemba Walk report uses a category-based system to organize observations by department or area. The system provides default categories for common departments:

- Human Resources
- Finance  
- Production
- Quality

You can use these preset default categories or add your own custom categories based on the areas you're observing during your Gemba Walk.

To add a category, click the plus button. You can select from the default categories or create a custom category name for specific areas you want to observe.

Each category can include:
- Observation statements that describe what you've observed
- Additional details with descriptions and tags
- Attachments like photos taken during the walk
- Assignees responsible for that area

To add observations to a category, click on the category card. You can then add statements that describe your observations. For Gemba Walk reports, statements are text-based observations rather than scored items.

You can document observations such as:
- Visual management practices observed
- 5S implementation status
- Safety compliance and concerns
- Process efficiency observations
- Employee engagement and morale
- Improvement opportunities identified

In the Additional Details section for each category, you can:
- Add detailed descriptions of your observations
- Upload photos taken during the Gemba Walk
- Tag relevant people or areas (as text notes)

You can rearrange categories using the rearrange button to organize them in the order that matches your walk route or priority.

The Gemba Walk report doesn't display a radar chart since it focuses on qualitative observations rather than quantitative scoring.

You can switch between vertical and horizontal layouts for better viewing of your categories and observations.

The system supports both highlights and department views, allowing you to organize information in the way that best suits your Gemba Walk process.

Finally, you can export to PDF and get yourself a comprehensive Gemba Walk report documenting all observations, photos, and action items identified during your walk.

The PDF export provides a professional format perfect for sharing with leadership and tracking improvement opportunities identified during the Gemba Walk.

That's it for the Gemba Walk report. It's an excellent tool for capturing field observations and driving continuous improvement through direct observation.
