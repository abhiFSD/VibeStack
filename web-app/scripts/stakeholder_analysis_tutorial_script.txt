Now let's get started with the Stakeholder Analysis report. 

The Stakeholder Analysis report helps you identify, analyze, and plan engagement strategies for all stakeholders involved in or affected by your project or initiative.

At the top, you'll see the Action Items section for tracking stakeholder engagement tasks and communication plans.

**Data Entry Interface**
Below that, you'll find a data table for analyzing individual stakeholders:

Click "Add" to create new stakeholder entries. For each stakeholder, you'll provide:
- **Label**: The name or title of the stakeholder (person, group, or organization)
- **Value**: Influence/power score or priority level
- **Description**: Role, interests, concerns, and engagement approach

**Stakeholder Identification**
The analysis helps you identify different types of stakeholders:
- **Internal Stakeholders**: Employees, management, departments, teams
- **External Stakeholders**: Customers, suppliers, regulators, community
- **Primary Stakeholders**: Directly affected by the project
- **Secondary Stakeholders**: Indirectly affected or influential

Export your Stakeholder Analysis to PDF, including:
- Complete stakeholder inventory with influence scores
- Engagement strategies and communication plans
- Risk assessment and mitigation approaches
- Action plan for stakeholder management

The PDF provides professional documentation for project planning, change management, and stakeholder engagement activities.
